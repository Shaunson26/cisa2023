Isabel Sanmartín

Real Jardín Botánico de Madrid

Dr Isabel Sanmartín is a senior scientist at the Real Jardín Botánico de Madrid (Consejo Superior de Investigaciones Científicas), where she is also vicedirector of scientific culture. Dr Sanmartín’s research focuses on studying the evolutionary and ecological processes behind general patterns of biodiversity distribution, with special focus on endemism, rarity and geographic disjunctions. She uses model-based approaches, such as Bayesian Inference, to integrate multiple sources of evidence, from phylogenetic and genomic data, to geographic databases, the fossil record, and paleoclimatic reconstructions. Her latest projects focus on understanding the role of climatic extinction at deep-time and microevolutionary time scales, and their effect at community levels, such as the observed pattern of species depauperation in African lineages compared to their Neotropical counterpart. She is currently exploring the use of machine learning (deep neural) approaches to recover patterns of biotic assembly at different phylogenetic levels, from extinction and adaptation.
