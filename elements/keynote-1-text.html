Michelle Leger

Institut de Biologia Evolutiva (CSIC-UPF), Barcelona

Dr Michelle Leger is a postdoctoral researcher at the Institut de Biologia Evolutiva (CSIC-UPF), Barcelona, currently holding a Juan de la Cierva Incorporaci√≥n grant. Her research interests are related to protists and the origin and evolution of eukaryotes, addressing one of the most important evolutionary transitions in the history of life: the origins of multicellularity. She has devoted her career to reconstruct the nature of the unicellular ancestor that gave rise to animals by performing comparative and functional genomic analyses between animals and their closest unicellular relatives. Dr Leger is also interested in providing a timing in the events in the evolution of eukaryotic cells using transcriptomics, genomics and proteomics. She has also worked on the function that Mitochondrion-related organelles (organelles that have independently evolved from mitochondria) play in eukaryotes that live in low-oxygen conditions.
